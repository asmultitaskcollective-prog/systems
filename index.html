Fit check

Three signals this is the right conversation.

If any of these are recurring — not just occasional — the issue is structural, not operational.

Work keeps routing back to the same two people.

The system depends on memory, private context, or a specific person being available. When they're not, things stall or get missed.

Records exist, but nobody fully trusts them.

The CRM has data but the team doesn't rely on it for decisions. Fields are outdated, stages don't reflect reality, owners are wrong.

Automation works until the first exception.

Workflows run fine in the happy path. The moment something unusual happens, there's no fallback — just a missed step and a manual fix.

Common questions

Frequently asked.

What tools do you work in?+
The work happens inside whatever the business already uses. Common platforms include GoHighLevel, HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, Zapier, Make, Airtable, Notion, ClickUp, Monday.com, ActiveCampaign, Zendesk, and Google Workspace. If your stack isn't listed, bring it to the audit — the overlap is usually broader than expected.
How long does a typical engagement take?+
A focused repair of one area — a single pipeline, one broken workflow, a documentation gap — typically runs two to four weeks. Multi-area rebuilds depend on scope and are mapped clearly before any work begins. The first step is always the audit, which gives a realistic timeline for whatever the diagnosis finds.
We're a small team. Is this built for us?+
Most engagements are with teams of three to twenty people — businesses with real volume but without a dedicated operations or systems hire. The work is built to match that scale: practical, not enterprise-heavy, and documented so the team can maintain it without ongoing outside support.
Our situation is complicated. We have a mix of old systems and new ones.+
Complicated is fine — it's common. The audit is specifically built to untangle mixed environments before any build decisions are made. The map that comes out of it distinguishes between what can be repaired, what needs to be replaced, and what can stay as-is. You won't be pushed toward a full rebuild if a targeted fix handles it.
Do you offer ongoing support after the project is done?+
The deliverable always includes documentation and a handoff designed for the team to own the system going forward. For teams that want continued support — quarterly reviews, additional workflow builds, or training — that's available as a separate conversation after the initial engagement closes.
What does the intake form actually do?+
It opens your email client with the intake pre-filled. Nothing is sent through a third-party server. Your data goes directly from your device to the email — no forms backend, no storage. It's kept simple intentionally.
